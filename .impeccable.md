## Design Context

### Users
This portfolio is first a living archive for Sarwin Chandran, built to hold work across physics, programming, art, astrophotography, poetry, music, and reflective writing without flattening them into a resume. It should also work well for professors and academic readers who need to quickly understand how technical rigor, creative practice, and personal reflection connect across the body of work.

### Brand Personality
Physicist. Artist. Programmer.

The site should feel observant, disciplined, and personal at the same time. It should evoke curiosity, trust, intellectual seriousness, and warmth. The tone should read like an academic field journal with a human voice, not a generic portfolio template and not a startup landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is an editorial archive with warm paper tones in light mode, deep observatory tones in dark mode, strong typography, annotated layouts, and clear structural rhythm. The portfolio should remain multi-page, but the framing should feel more like "Discover Me" than "Field Journal." The primary page set is: Physics, Code, Art, Captures, Creatives, and Blog. It should work well across mobile, tablet, and desktop.

Anti-reference:
Do not make this look like a generic portfolio, AI-gradient landing page, glassy dashboard, or interchangeable "creative dev" template.

### Design Principles
1. Treat every page like part of one shared archive, not a set of disconnected category screens.
2. Balance academic clarity with personal texture so professors can evaluate the work and the person behind it.
3. Give each medium its own accent and pacing while preserving a unified visual language.
4. Favor strong hierarchy, readable copy, accessible interactions, and responsive layouts over decorative excess.
5. Make the site feel intentional and memorable without relying on trendy portfolio clichés.
